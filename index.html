Immediate Danger Signs (High Risk)

Read the situation and decide: HIGH RISK or NOT? Select the card to check your answer and learn why.

HIGH RISK or NOT?
A patron on the phone says, "If you leave, you will pay. I will kill you before I let you take the kids."
YES, IT IS HIGH RISK.

Why? Any threat to kill is a life-threatening risk. It indicates an immediate emergency and a strong intent to harm.

Action: Your job is to immediately follow club policy and call the police (000) or security to ensure the victim-survivor's safety.

HIGH RISK or NOT?
A staff member quietly tells their manager: "My partner put their hands around my neck and squeezed until I couldn't breathe."
YES, IT IS HIGH RISK.

Why? Strangulation (choking) is a critical danger sign. It is one of the highest risk factors for a person being killed in the future, even if there are no visible injuries.

Action: You must immediately connect the staff member with specialist services (like 1800RESPECT) and secure their safety at work. Immediate referral is needed.

HIGH RISK or NOT?
A staff member says: "My partner makes me give him all my payslips and I have to ask him for money just to buy lunch."
NO, BUT IT IS STILL ABUSE.

Why? This is Economic Abuse. It is controlling and harmful but does not have the critical fatality factors (threats to kill, strangulation) that require a 000 emergency response.

Action: This person needs immediate help and a specialist referral (e.g., EAP, 1800RESPECT). Always offer workplace support (like DV leave).
